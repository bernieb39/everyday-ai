Questions people actually ask

Why is it free? What's the catch?

No catch — just timing. I'm at the start of this venture, and before I charge anyone I want a site full of real tools built for real people. You get a genuinely useful tool and free support; I get a great example to show here (anonymized, and only with your OK). When I do start charging, early users keep what they have.

I don't understand AI at all. Is that a problem?

It's the whole point. You never touch the AI — you describe a problem in plain words and use the finished tool like you'd use a kettle. If a tool of mine ever needs a manual, I've built it wrong.

How can one person build these things so quickly?

Because I use AI to build them — that's the honest trick. What used to take a software team a month, one experienced builder directing AI can do in days. What I bring is the judgment about what to build and the guarantee that it works, not a thousand hours of typing.

What if I don't know what I need?

Most people don't — that's normal. In the free chat I'll ask about your week and listen for the sentence that starts with “and then every Sunday I have to…”. That sentence is usually the product.

Is my information safe?

Your tool is built for you alone: your data isn't shared, sold, or mixed with anyone else's, and wherever possible the tool runs on your own accounts. Anything sensitive — finances, health, kids — we talk through explicitly before I build.

What happens when something breaks?

You tell me and I fix it — free, like everything else right now. Build & Care tools I monitor myself, so most problems are fixed before you notice them.

What can't AI do?

Plenty. It won't replace your lawyer's, doctor's, or accountant's judgment — tools like the letter writer and contract explainer prepare you, they don't advise you. And some ideas just aren't worth building; when yours is one, I'll say so at the chat stage, for free, instead of selling you a project.

Tell me what's eating your time

Three sentences about your most annoying weekly chore is a perfect first message. I'll reply with what I'd build and whether it's worth building — and right now, building it costs you nothing.
